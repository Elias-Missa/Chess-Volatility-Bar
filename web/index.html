          Eval tells you who's winning. Volatility tells you how hard it is to play.

          Stockfish's eval ranks positions by best play. It can't see that one of two equally "equal" positions is a bone-dry handshake while the other is a single-move minefield. The volatility bar measures that difference: how punishing it is to choose the wrong move.

          What V is not

          • V is not difficulty. A knife-edge position is V-high, but a strong player who has seen the pattern finds the move in seconds. V measures the cost of guessing wrong, not the cognitive load of guessing right.
          • V is not a blunder predictor. It tells you what a blunder would cost, not how likely either side is to make one.
          • V is not a style score. "Sharp" and "quiet" describe openings; V answers — how punishing is the wrong move right now?
